# Stage-1 de-risk gate (P7) — certified k vs empirical robustness

Generated by `scripts/make_stage1_report.py`. Tests H1: does the
deletion certificate `k` predict robustness to the RAG-Safety insertion
attack? For MetaQA 2-hop queries stratified by `k`, the reasoner answers
from clean supporting evidence and from evidence + a forged competing
chain to a type-consistent wrong answer. **Flip rate** = fraction of
*clean-correct* queries whose answer flips to the forged wrong answer
under attack. Prediction: flip rate falls as `k` rises (more independent
real support resists a forged path).

## Decision

GO if certified (k>=1) queries are markedly more robust than k=0 and a
non-trivial fraction of queries admit k>=1 (certificate_distribution.md:
MetaQA 2-hop 31%, 3-hop 97%, 2Wiki 68%). Otherwise PIVOT to the
certificate-maximizing retriever (P8). Decision recorded in DECISIONS.md.
