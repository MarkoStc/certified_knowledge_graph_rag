## The finding (H1)

Certified redundancy rises sharply and monotonically with hop count, and
is stable across dev/test:

- **1-hop → 0.5% certifiable.** A 1-hop answer is a single fact; there is essentially no independent second path, so `k = 0` almost everywhere. Also a correctness check.
- **2-hop → 31.3% (dev) / 31.4% (test).** A substantial minority of queries admit an independent second path.
- **3-hop → 96.9% (dev) / 96.7% (test).** Dense KB regions yield many edge-disjoint supporting paths.

This monotonic hop-count → path-redundancy relationship, on full splits
(~10k-15k queries each) with tight dev/test agreement, is the core P7
signal and the empirical basis of the §12 hop-count vs path-count ablation.

## Scope & caveats (AGENTS.md §1)

- Datasets so far need no Freebase / no gated model (MetaQA self-contained;
  2Wiki via public Wikidata). WebQSP/CWQ await the Freebase endpoint.
- `k` counts *structural* edge-disjoint paths in the bounded k-hop
  subgraph; paths inside the ball may exceed `hops` edges, so `k` partly
  reflects local KB connectivity. Structural ≠ evidential independence
  (P5) — two edge-disjoint paths can still share a corrupted source. The
  2Wiki hub-pruning is a first, partial control for this.
- Certificate is conservative: parallel facts are collapsed to one edge,
  and (2Wiki) only gold-chain entities' claims are fetched — both can only
  lower `k`.
