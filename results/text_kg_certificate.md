Generated by `scripts/compute_text_kg_certificates.py` (n=3000 certifiable
bridge questions, distractor dev; 318 yes/no and 1484 no-anchor

## Reading

The certificate machinery **generalizes to text-constructed KGs**: it
computes end-to-end with no curated graph, and a non-trivial share of
questions carry genuine path redundancy (certified k>=1) purely from
Wikipedia co-mentions. Coverage is lower than the dense curated KBs
(MetaQA 3-hop 97%), as expected — HotpotQA supplies only the minimal
supporting context, so many co-mention KGs are single bridge chains
(k=0). This locates the text regime as the one where the
certificate-maximizing retriever (P8) — pulling additional independent
supporting passages — has the most headroom. Richer extraction
(entity-level NER, more retrieved passages) would raise it further.
